Competition Format

PRS-style precision shooting with timed stages

What is PRS?

PRS (Precision Rifle Series) is a competition format that challenges shooters with multiple props, varied distances, and time limits. TAC12 adapts this proven format for the UK's <12 ft/lbs PCP Air Rifles.

Competition Structure

🎯

Multi-Prop Stages

Each stage presents unique challenges with different props and shooting positions to keep competitors on their toes.

📏

20m - 90m Distances

Targets placed at varying distances from 20 meters out to 90 meters test your hold, wind reading, and ballistics knowledge.

⏱️

Timed Stages

Stages are shot against the clock, adding pressure and excitement while testing your speed and accuracy under stress.

Shooting Positions

PRS stages use multiple positions to challenge your skills:

  • Bench Rest - Stable supported position for precision
  • Prone - Ground level shooting position
  • Standing - Unsupported freestyle position
  • Positional Challenges - Creative props requiring unconventional positions

Equipment Rules

⚠️ Important

Springers are welcome for training sessions, but PRS competition stages must be shot with a magazine-fed PCP Airgun.

Entry Requirements

Requirement Specification
Airgun Type PCP, magazine fed (10+ shots minimum)
Optic Telescopic (FFP preferred, not essential)
Caliber .22 cal or .177 cal
Ability 1.5 mil group size
Power Limit Under 12 ft/lbs
